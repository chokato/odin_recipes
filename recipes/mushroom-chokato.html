Mushroom and Chokato Soup

Earthy and enchanting, this soup pairs the deep, savory flavors of mushrooms with the curious sweetness of Chokatos, creating a dish that feels like wandering through the Haunted Woods on a crisp autumn day. It’s rich, velvety, and just the right amount of peculiar—perfect for curious Neopians with adventurous taste buds!

Ingredients

  • 4 Chokatos, peeled and chopped
  • 2 cups Wild Mushrooms, sliced
  • 3 cups Woodland Spring Water
  • 1 cup Mootix Milk
  • 1 small Onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves Garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon Midnight Thyme
  • A pinch of Shadowroot Pepper

Steps

  1. In a cauldron-like pot, sauté the onion and garlic in a drizzle of Woodland Spring Water until fragrant and golden.
  2. Add the mushrooms and cook until they release their earthy aroma, transforming into a foresty feast for the senses.
  3. Stir in the chopped Chokatos, letting their sweet and tangy notes blend with the mushrooms.
  4. Pour in the remaining Woodland Spring Water and sprinkle in Midnight Thyme and Shadowroot Pepper. Simmer until the soup thickens and the flavors meld like magic.
  5. Stir in Mootix Milk for a creamy finish, allowing the soup to become irresistibly velvety.
  6. Garnish with a sprinkle of extra thyme.
