Lotus Root Soup

Description

The combination of lotus root with pork ribs and peanuts is a classic Chinese soup. In Cantonese, we call it ling ngau tong (莲藕汤).

Lotus root signifies abundance and prosperity to the Chinese so this is a popular soup for many who celebrate Chinese New Year.

Recipe adapted from Souper Diaries

Ingredients

Serves 4

  • 1 long lotus root (about 500g)
  • 500g pork ribs
  • 100g peanuts
  • 1 carrot — peeled and cut into chunks
  • 6-8 red dates
  • 1 dried cuttlefish — rinsed (optional)
  • 4 dried scallops — softened in a bowl of hot water for about 10-15 minutes (optional)
  • Water
  • Salt

Instructions

  1. Soak peanuts in hot boiling water for about 30 minutes. Discard the soaking water.
  2. Blanch pork ribs in a small pot of boiling water for about 5 minutes.
  3. Wash, peel, and cut the lotus roots into 1cm slices.
  4. Put in all the ingredients in a soup pot. Add in enough water to submerge all the ingredients by 1 to 2 inches.
  5. Bring to a boil and let it simmer on the lowest heat for 2-3 hours until the meat is tender.
  6. Season with salt to tasteand serve hot.
